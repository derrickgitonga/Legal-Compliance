Statutory Framework

Digital escrow operates under a robust web of commercial laws protecting both buyers and sellers.

Chapter 23, Laws of Kenya

Law of Contract Act

Milestones and terms initiated on Funga-Deal form legally binding contracts. Contractual obligations must be fulfilled exactly as agreed before any funds are released.

Act No. 2 of 1998

Kenya Information & Communications Act

KICA validates electronic records and digital signatures. A digital agreement on Funga-Deal holds the exact same evidentiary weight as a physical signature in a Kenyan court.

Act No. 46 of 2012

Consumer Protection Act

This Act shields consumers from fraudulent vendors. By utilizing the escrow model, buyers are guaranteed not to pay for defective goods or undelivering merchants.

Act No. 24 of 2019

Data Protection Act

All PII (Personally Identifiable Information) and transaction data are processed strictly per the law. Funga-Deal ensures localized, encrypted, and transparent data handling.

Licensing & Platform Operations

The regulatory and operational licenses required to run a legally compliant escrow in Kenya.

Regulatory Compliance

CBK Payment Service Provider

To hold user funds, escrow services must be partnered with or licensed by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) as a Payment Service Provider (PSP). Funga-Deal ensures all financial routing happens via licensed banking or mobile money APIs (such as M-PESA).

Platform Usage

End-User License Agreement

Users are granted a revocable, non-exclusive license to use the platform. The Terms of Service strictly prohibit illicit trades, and Funga-Deal retains the right to freeze accounts involved in breaches of Kenyan commercial law.

Digital Assets

Intellectual Property (IP) Transfer

For transactions involving digital goods, Funga-Deal enforces the legal transfer of IP. The buyer only receives the full commercial license to the asset once the escrow funds are irrevocably cleared and released to the seller.
