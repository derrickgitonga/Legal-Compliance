Statutory Framework

Digital escrow operates under a robust web of commercial laws protecting both buyers and sellers.

Chapter 23, Laws of Kenya

Law of Contract Act

Milestones and terms initiated on Funga-Deal form legally binding contracts. Contractual obligations must be fulfilled exactly as agreed before any funds are released.

Act No. 2 of 1998

Kenya Information & Communications Act

KICA validates electronic records and digital signatures. A digital agreement on Funga-Deal holds the exact same evidentiary weight as a physical signature in a Kenyan court.

Act No. 46 of 2012

Consumer Protection Act

This Act shields consumers from fraudulent vendors. By utilizing the escrow model, buyers are guaranteed not to pay for defective goods or undelivering merchants.

Act No. 24 of 2019

Data Protection Act

All PII (Personally Identifiable Information) and transaction data are processed strictly per the law. Funga-Deal ensures localized, encrypted, and transparent data handling.

How Transactions are Secured

The structured workflow that enforces trust.

01

Digital Agreement

Parties agree to terms digitally. This step instantly forms a binding contract recognized under KICA.

02

Escrow Funding

Buyer deposits funds. The money is secured in a designated escrow account, held by a CBK-regulated partner.

03

Service Delivery

Seller delivers the agreed-upon goods or services. Milestones are tracked and verified transparently.

04

Legitimate Release

Upon buyer approval and satisfaction, funds are released to the seller, completing the contractual loop.
